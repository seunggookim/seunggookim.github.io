Seung-Goo Kim, PhD

Research Scientist at RG-NCML, Max Planck Institute for Empirical Aesthetics

Seung-Goo [IPA:sɯŋgu] KIM (김승구;金勝九) is a cognitive neuroscientist specialized in human neuroimaging and auditory processing. Seung-Goo has been using functional/structural magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) and M/EEG source imaging to investigate how human brain processes sounds and music and how they lead to the evocation of intense emotions.

After a Ph.D. program at Max Planck Institute for Human Cognitive and Brain Sciences, Germany in 2017 (PI: Dr. Thomas R. Knösche), he has worked as a postdoc at the University of Cambridge (PI: Dr. Valerie Voon), Duke University (PI: Dr. Tobias Overath), and currently as a research scientist at Max Planck Institute for Empirical Aesthetics (PI: Dr. Daniela Sammler).
